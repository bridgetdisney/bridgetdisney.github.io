Research Interests

Data science education for librarians, data curation (including metadata and dataset discovery), research assistance services, data visualization, text analysis, data mining, and digital humanities.

Research Philosophy: I approach research from a pragmatic world view, considering what works for collecting data and understanding the problem.

Dissertation Topic: Dataset Discovery

Research question: How can librarians help researchers find relevant datasets?

Finding dataset references in published papers

This research would expand upon previous attemps to extract dataset information from published papers. Different strategies would be examined and new possibilities explored.

Using topic modeling of datasets to enhance metadata

Topic modeling would be used to enhance metadata information about datasets. This would include analyzing field names as well as field content.

Analyzing decisisions for dataset use and reuse

This could be a case study of five papers, each representing different disciplines, where the author searched for and reused datasets. The specific challenges encountered using and integrating existing data would be covered as well as the advantages. The purpose would be to help research librarians understand how to bests serve patrons with research and data curation needs.
